Game Casserole

High Protein, Easy-To-Make Easy-to-Shop Casserole

This recipe is quick, easy-to-make and (something I don't often find included in online recipes) easy-to-shop for. You can fit everything you need to buy for this recipe into one backpack or tote. Pedestrians and 90 lb weaklings rejoice!

The protein I opted for is Pheasant and Venison, mainly because they come pre-packaged together in the supermarket. Feel free to use whatever meat or protein source you prefer, given it can withstand a relatively long cooking time. And please note, this recipe includes alcohol!

Want or need to sub-out alcohol? Try vinegars instead!

Without further ado, let's get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 250g Pheasant
  • 250g Venison
  • 2 Carrots
  • 1 Parsnip
  • 1/2 of a Butternut Squash
  • 7 or so Shallots
  • 2 or 3 Potatoes
  • Sage
  • Thyme
  • Approx 1 teaspoon Juniper Berries
  • 4 cubes of Vegetable Stock
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons Redcurrant Jelly orCranberry Sauce
  • 2 or so cups of Flour/Cornflour
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 2 glasses of Red Wine (Malbec/Rioja/Chianti will do)
  • Quite literally any cooking oil

Prep

While you could do some of these steps whilst cooking, I find it personally brain-breaking. So here's what to do first!

  • If not done already, dice your meat into approximately 1 inch cubes, on a seperate surface to your vegetables (or sanitize your prep surfaces after cutting meat). Toss the meat with Salt, Pepper and Flour to coat.
  • Slice your carrots into rounds.
  • Cube the parsnip and squash quite small, as these are dense starchy vegetables and tend to take longer to cook. Think Thumbnail sized.
  • Quarter your shallots.
  • Peel and slice your potatoes. You want enough slices to cover your casserole dish.
  • Boil a kettle and make up 300 ml of stock using the aforementioned stock cubes.

Actual Cooking

  1. Heat oil in a pan on a medium high heat. Once hot, add the shallots and cook until browned, then remove.
  2. Reoil the pan if needed, then add the meat (but not all the flour if it's swimming in it, come on) and brown.
  3. Once the meat is browned, add the 2 glasses of red wine, 300ml stock, carrots, parsnip, squash, the browned shallots, sage, thyme, juniper berries, and your redcurrant/cranberry sauce. Once you reach a boil, turn the heat down and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer to a casserole dish, top with the potatoes and cook on 180Â°C (fan) for 90 minutes. You should be good to eat once the topping potatoes are crisped up.
