Prawn Pathia

Sweet! Sour! Curry!

Another quick, easy-to-make and easy-to-shop for recipe. You can fit everything you need to buy for this recipe into one backpack or tote easy.

Without further ado, let's get cooking!

Ingredients

  • Prawns
  • Naans. Cornershop ones are usually better than supermarket ones. Or be fancy and make your own, but I won't tell you how to, because I don't know how to do that.
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground Turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon Cayenne Pepper
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Powder
  • 1 tablespoon Curry Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tablespoon Fresh Ginger
  • 4 or 5 Garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon Tamarind Paste
  • 2 & 1/2 tablespoons Tomato Paste
  • 1 Onion
  • 100g or so Basmati Rice
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Quite literally any cooking oil

Prep

While you could do some of these steps whilst cooking, I find it personally brain-breaking. So here's what to do first!

  • Peel and finely chop the onion, garlic and ginger.
  • Preheat your oven to 180Â°C (fan), and cook the naans.
  • Boil a kettle and set aside 150 ml of water.

Actual Cooking

  1. Heat oil in a wide base pan on a medium heat. Once hot, add the onions with a pinch of salt and cook until softened.
  2. Simultaneously, heat another pot over medium heat, and add the cumin seeds once hot. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the seeds are toasted, but not burnt. Then, add the rice and stir. Add the turmeric and 250 ml cold water, again with a pinch of salt, and bring to the boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and cook, covered, for 10-12 minutes/until the rice is cooked. Once cooked, remove from heat but keep covered.
  3. While the rice is cooking and once the onions have softened, add the ginger and garlic and cook until fragrant (1-2 minutes).
  4. In the curry pan add the ground cumin, curry powder, cayenne pepper and tomato paste and stir.
  5. Again, in the curry pan add the tamarind paste with your boiled water and approximately 2 teaspoons of sugar. Stir again and adjust salt/sugar to taste.
  6. FInally, add the prawns to the curry pot and cook until they are cooked through and you are satisfied with the thickness of the curry. Serve with rice and naans.
