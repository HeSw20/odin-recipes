Rice and Beans

Easy not-quite-risotto to terrify your friends

I made this recipe once for a friend and made the mistake of calling it rice and peas. I have since been informed it is nothing of the sort. Still, I like it!

Again, this recipe is easy to shop for. You should be able to get pretty much all of the ingredients at a cornershop, let alone a supermarket, and it's not too heavy to carry home.

Without further ado, let's get cooking!

Ingredients

  • Rice, I use Basmati
  • 1 can Coconut Milk
  • 1 can (yes, can) beans of your choice. I like gungo or blackeye peas. I also like not spending my freetime rehydrating beans, so I get a can and have done with it.
  • 1 Onion
  • 2 or so Vegetable Stock Cubes
  • All Purpose Seasoning
  • 1 or 2 chillies, fresh or dried. I like Jamacian Jerk or Lemon Drop variety.
  • Quite literally any cooking oil

Prep

Not too much for this one!

  • Dice your Onion.
  • Dice your chilli.
  • Boil a kettle and make up 400 ml of stock using the aforementioned stock cubes.

Actual Cooking

  1. Heat oil in a pan on a medium heat. Once hot, add the onions and cook until sweated (translucent).
  2. Add your all purpose seasoning and chilli and fry off until fragrant (like a minute or so).
  3. Add the coconut milk. When the pot is up to a simmer, add the stock, rice and beans.
  4. Cover the pot and cook on low until the rice is cooked. You might need to drain the rice at the end (quelle horror!)
